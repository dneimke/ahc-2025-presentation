Team Spirit

Timing hint: 60–80 seconds

Grab the audience's attention immediately, setting a tone of resilience, unity, and club spirit right from the start.

Opening Cue:

Talk about Shaun ‘Dawso’ Dawson dislocating his shoulder in the First Final.

  • Describe the scene on the field (what it looked like).
  • The key moment: Lying on the ground, still singing the club song before the ambulance. (Heartfelt and funny detail).

Resilience Cue:

Share how Eddie played through finals with an injury that should have kept him out—he didn't just play, he doubled up and then won player of the final!

Highlight Hildo managing hamstring issues—he played a key role at training, provided valuable feedback, and stepped up as assistant coach when unable to play.

Mention Macca managing soft tissue injuries, making tough calls about when he needed to be on the pitch versus when to focus on recovery.

Character Cue:

Highlight Hildo vs Haynes as our "hype man" role—always bringing energy and lifting the group before big moments.

Talk about Orlando and Ollie — two key players all year. After a tough conversation on the night of the final training, they learned they wouldn't get the opportunity to play in the final. Both took it with incredible maturity and sat on the bench during the final, supporting and experiencing it with the team. Amazing humans, great talent.

Season & Culture (Unity and Belief)

Timing hint: 2.5–3 minutes

This combined section highlights how the team’s unity and belief drove them through challenges and how the club culture, lessons and leadership turned that into success.

Unity & Growth: "We were stronger together" — "We learned to dig deep..."

  • Unity: Every player, whether on the field or supporting from the sidelines, contributed to the team’s spirit. Moments of teamwork where players lifted each other and pushed through adversity as one group.
  • Growth: Share a moment where the team had to rally together (mental resilience):
    • 2 of our 3 losses were consecutive against top two teams
    • Scored 3 second half goals against Port
    • Took that momentum into the North East game (still many players out)
  • Personal Sacrifices: Macca from airport to North East; players doubling up and managing significant injuries.

Belief: "We always believed in each other"

This success is built on the belief instilled by Gouldy's leadership last year.

  • Adversity Cue: "We faced challenges head-on." Remember to mention injuries and how the team handled tough opponents.
  • Tough games (specific examples):
    • Woodville — come back to draw 3-3 (last few minutes goal)
    • Burnside — 5-4 (last minute goal)
    • Seacliff — 5-5 (last second goal)
    • Burnside — 0-1 (last 2 minute goal to go to extra time)
    • Port GF — 0-2 4 minutes before half time (final score 4-2)
  • Unshakeable Belief: Even when we looked down and out in so many games, there was always this underlying feeling among players and supporters that we'd find a way through. That quiet confidence—shared in conversations and moments on the field—kept us pushing, no matter the odds.

Acknowledgements

Timing hint: 40–60 seconds

A crucial part of the speech dedicated to delivering specific, important thanks to the wider club community, staff, and management who made huge sacrifices and provided unwavering support.

Club Support & Key Contributors

  • Metro 5 Men's (The Phabs) Cue: Thank them for their unwavering support, mentioning the BBQs and their presence at the final Thursday night sessions.
  • Premier League Men's Cue: Thank them for their support through the finals, acknowledging them training with and pushing us to the end.
  • Metro Two Men's Cue: Thank Mike ‘Chook’ Angley and his team, acknowledging the disruption to their team and mentioning Gobbo who stepped up (playing and supporting from off-field).
  • Rich Tyrer: Acknowledge his role in selections and our journey across the entire year (in addition to coaching the M4M).
  • Stephen ‘Vausey’ Vause (Special Thank You): We simply couldn't have done it without Steve and his dedication and unwavering support.
    • Mention his incredible dedication, attending every training.
    • Highlight the behind-the-scenes connection: Shared a drink after every game (win, lose or draw) for reflection.

Conclusion

Timing hint: 20–30 seconds

The final, powerful message to drive home the ultimate achievement and leave the audience with a profound sense of shared success and pride.

Final Statement Cue:

"The win is a testament not only to the hard work and dedication of everyone here, but to the bonds and personal connections we forged and reinforced along the way."

  • Reiterate the team's achievements and what made them champions.
  • End with a **strong, memorable statement** (e.g., "And that's what made us champions.")

Full timed script & delivery tips (expand)
Script (5:30 target)

Opener (0:00–0:55)
"I want to start with Shaun ‘Dawso’ Dawson — who dislocated his shoulder in the first final…and sang the club song while waiting for the ambulance. That sums up the character of this group."

Unity & Growth (0:55–2:30)
"We were stronger together. We learned to dig deep. After two tough losses we came back, scored three second-half goals against Port, and pushed through despite injuries and sacrifices like Macca’s airport run."

Belief & Gouldy (2:30–4:20)
"We always believed in each other. We faced challenges head-on — grit in games like Woodville (3-3 comeback) and that last-minute Burnside win. And it’s the leadership, people like Gouldy, that built that belief and kept showing up."

Acknowledgements (4:20–5:10)
"Thank you to the Phabs and the club supporters, to Chook and the staff who stepped up. We simply couldn't have done it without Steve and his dedication and unwavering support."

Conclusion (5:10–5:30)
"This win belongs to everyone here — those who sacrificed, believed, and showed up. It also celebrates the bonds and personal connections we forged and reinforced together. Let’s celebrate them."

Delivery tips
  • Pace: aim for ~140–160 words/min. Pause 1–2s after key lines.
  • Use contrast: quieter setup, louder payoff.
  • Eye contact and gestures: point to groups when thanking them.
  • Rehearse with the timer and cut one example if over time.
Quick cuts (if you need to shorten)
  • Drop one game example.
  • Shrink acknowledgements to 2 items.
  • Trim the opener by removing one sub-example.
